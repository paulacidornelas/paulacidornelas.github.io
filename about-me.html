About Me

Hi! I'm Paula, a PhD student in Civil Engineering at the University of British Columbia, specializing in Transportation. Originally from Brasília, Brazil, I moved to Vancouver in 2022 to pursue my master's degree and fell in love with the city (despite the rain!).

My Research Journey

I've always had a passion for mathematics and engineering, but something felt missing until I discovered how transportation could directly impact society. This realization allowed me to find my path—leveraging technical skills to drive positive change.

Currently, my research focuses on sustainable pavement management systems, exploring ways to create infrastructure that is both climate resilient and has minimal environmental emissions.

Life Outside Academia

When I'm not doing research, you'll likely find me with my nose in a book. I love reading fiction, with Elena Ferrante and Isabel Allende ranking among my favorites. One of my favorite quotes about reading is: "Read non-fiction to raise your floor. Read fiction to raise your ceiling."

Since moving to Vancouver, I've been embracing the Pacific Northwest lifestyle by doing yoga, exploring hiking trails, and trying to ski.

Looking Forward

My goal is to make impactful contributions to sustainable and equitable transportation systems through my research. While the specifics of my future path are still unfolding, my purpose is clear: to identify common ground and develop economically viable, sustainable solutions that benefit all communities, especially those historically underrepresented.

I see transportation not just as infrastructure, but as a critical element of social equity and environmental sustainability. By bridging technical expertise with policy insights, I hope to help shape transportation systems that work for everyone.

If you'd like to chat about transportation research, book recommendations, or anything else mentioned here, feel free to reach out via email or LinkedIn. I'm always happy to connect!
